Conceptual Overview

The Structural Plenitude Theory (SPT) proposes a continuous, viscoelastic substrate permeating all spatial layers, governing radiative, thermal, inertial, and gravitational interactions. Unlike field-based frameworks, SPT focuses on mechanical continuity and substrate-driven anisotropies that emerge from pressure, tension, impedance, and turbulent coupling within the cosmic medium.

Core Principles

  • The universe is embedded in a layered, mechanically continuous substrate.
  • Energy transfer is governed by viscoelastic stress, shear, and relaxation times.
  • Flows within the substrate generate apparent gravitational and radiative behavior.
  • Planetary and stellar asymmetries arise from substrate-turbulent interactions.
  • The SPT unifies radiative, thermal, and inertial phenomena through substrate mechanics.
