Mathematical Basis of SPT

The Structural Plenitude Theory models the universe as a continuous viscoelastic substrate whose internal state variables obey coupled mechanical relations.

1. Substrate State Variables

The substrate is represented by:

  • Local pressure field \( P(x,t) \)
  • Viscoelastic stress tensor \( \sigma_{ij} \)
  • Shear modulus \( G \)
  • Bulk modulus \( K \)
  • Relaxation time \( \tau \)

2. Fundamental Stress Relation

A first-order approximation of substrate stress is: 

σ_ij = K θ δ_ij + 2G ε_ij + η ∂ε_ij/∂t

where:

  • \( θ \) is the volumetric strain
  • \( ε_{ij} \) is the shear strain tensor
  • \( η \) is the effective viscosity of the substrate

3. Effective Gravitational Behavior

Gravitational accelerations in SPT emerge from large-scale gradients of internal substrate impedance \( Z \): 

g = -∇Z

This describes gravity not as a curvature, but as a mechanical descent along impedance gradients.
