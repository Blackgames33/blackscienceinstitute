The vacuum is not empty. It is a structured physical substrate — the Pleroma — where light, gravity and matter emerge as structural responses.

From Ether to Structure

Throughout the history of physics, the ether emerged as an attempt to reconcile wave propagation, inertial frames and the apparent continuity of space. Its failure was structural — it lacked physical definition, predictive equations and falsifiable dynamics.

Structural Plenitude Theory does not revive the ether. It resolves its historical limitations by redefining the vacuum as a viscoelastic substrate governed by coupling efficiency, structural impedance and dissipative regimes.

In SPT, the vacuum — the Pleroma — is not a reference frame. It is a physical structure whose effects emerge through interaction with matter and energy.
