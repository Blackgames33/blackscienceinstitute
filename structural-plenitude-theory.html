From Ether to Structure

From classical mechanics to relativistic cosmology, the vacuum has been treated primarily as geometry, metric structure, or fluctuation background — never as a fully characterized physical substrate.

The historical ether attempted to fill this conceptual void. It failed not because the intuition was entirely misplaced, but because it lacked a consistent covariant formalism and predictive structural dynamics.

Structural Plenitude Theory is not a revival of ether concepts — it is a structural reformulation of vacuum physics.

This framework does not reject relativistic geometry. It extends it into a physically reactive domain.
