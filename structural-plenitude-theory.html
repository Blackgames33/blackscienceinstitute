Editorial

Structural Plenitude Theory proposes that physical vacuum is not empty, but a continuous structural substrate — the Pleroma — capable of elastic response, dissipation and structural tension propagation.

Rather than replacing relativistic geometry, SPT extends it into a reactive physical domain, allowing integration of drag, anisotropic dissipation and coupling phenomena.

SPT is not a revival of historical ether concepts, but a structural reformulation of vacuum physics.

The theory emerged from accumulated observational patterns across astrophysics, plasma physics and interstellar object behavior, forming a unified structural interpretation framework.
