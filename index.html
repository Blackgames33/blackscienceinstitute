The Pleroma Substrate

Structural Plenitude Theory proposes that the quantum vacuum is an absolute physical medium with perfect viscoelastic properties. There is no empty space: there is continuous structural tension and residual dissipation.

Light, gravity, and cosmological redshift are emergent from this substrate. Objects like 3I/ATLAS reveal direct signatures of dynamic coupling — asymmetric anti-tail, jets, initial radio silence.

Pleroma Substrate

The Pleroma is not a medium nor a reference frame. It is a continuous physical substrate whose structural tensions give rise to light, gravity and inertia.

Viscoelastic Vacuum

In SPT, the vacuum behaves as a viscoelastic structure, exhibiting elastic anchoring, delayed response and dissipative regimes depending on coupling conditions.

3I/ATLAS Analysis
