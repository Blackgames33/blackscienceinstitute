ISP-Ni

Interstellar Signatures & Solar Perturbations

Statistical investigation of potential interstellar object signatures through historical anomaly reanalysis and heliospheric data correlation.

Editorial

The ISP-Ni program is grounded in a simple premise: not all observed signals have been fully characterized.

Historically, numerous radioastronomical events have been classified as noise, artifacts, or low-significance anomalies due to observational limitations and modeling constraints at the time of detection.

ISP-Ni does not seek to directly challenge these classifications — instead, it re-examines such events within a refined analytical framework, combining statistical validation, cross-instrument correlation, and trajectory simulation.

The objective is not to confirm a predefined hypothesis, but to evaluate whether statistically meaningful structures emerge beyond stochastic expectation.

Between signal and noise lies an underexplored domain. ISP-Ni operates precisely within that boundary.

Methodology

  • Independent radioastronomical catalog
  • Solar filtering
  • Residual anomaly extraction
  • Trajectory simulation
  • Statistical validation
