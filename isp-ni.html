ISP-Ni

Interstellar Signatures & Solar Perturbations

Statistical investigation of potential interstellar object signatures through historical anomaly reanalysis and heliospheric data correlation.

Overview

The discovery of interstellar objects such as ʻOumuamua and 2I/Borisov established that bodies originating from outside the Solar System traverse our local environment.

This shifts the problem: not only detecting new objects, but determining how many may have passed undetected.

ISP-Ni investigates historical anomalous events — signals detected but not fully explained — to evaluate whether they may be consistent with indirect signatures of interstellar object passage.

The project does not assume conclusions. It evaluates whether statistically significant correlations exist beyond random coincidence.

Anomaly Reassessment Framework

ISP-Ni systematically re-evaluates events historically classified as noise, artifacts, or unexplained anomalies.

These events are not assumed to be misinterpreted, but are analyzed under a new statistical and physical context.

Only signals exceeding statistical expectations beyond random coincidence are considered candidates for further investigation.

Methodology

  • Construction of independent radioastronomical event catalog
  • Filtering of known solar and heliospheric phenomena
  • Identification of residual unexplained events
  • Simulation of interstellar object trajectories
  • Temporal coincidence analysis
  • Monte Carlo statistical validation

Filtering and cross-correlation modules are under development.
