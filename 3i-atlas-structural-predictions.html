Prediction Window and Coupling Regimes

Jovian Impedance Gradient — March 2026

As 3I/ATLAS crosses the Jovian sector, SPT predicts a variation in structural impedance ZH due to the combined pleromic well of Jupiter and the Sun. This gradient modulates the local coupling efficiency η and may trigger subtle transitions in coma morphology and brightness.

  • Baseline laminar regime at large heliocentric distance.
  • Enhanced drag and possible brightening near the Jovian window.
  • Non-gravitational drift decoupled from classical mass-loss indicators.
  • Opportunity to compare antitail geometry before/after the gradient.

Coupling Efficiency η — Laminar vs. Non-Laminar

In SPT, radio silence is interpreted as a marker of efficient, laminar coupling: η ≈ 1 and ℛ ≈ 0. Any emergence of structured radio emission, sudden jet re-orientation or coma turbulence would signal a transition to non-laminar coupling.

  • Laminar regime: smooth antitail, radio quiet, stable morphology.
  • Non-laminar regime: localized tension release, jet asymmetry, possible transient radio features.
  • March 2026 monitoring focuses on whether η remains high across the Jovian sector.

Antitail, Residual Channel ℛ and Drift

Antitail as Substrate Drag and Momentum Lag

The persistent antitail of 3I/ATLAS is read as a momentum-lag artifact of substrate drag. The geometry of the antitail encodes how the nucleus interacts with the flow of the Pleroma rather than being purely driven by dust dynamics and phase angle effects.

Residual Radiative Channel ℛ and Non-Gravitational Drift

If part of the structural flux fails to anchor locally, SPT predicts a residual channel ℛ. In interstellar objects this may appear as a small but coherent drift not directly correlated with outgassing proxies.

  • Track MPC residuals against classical non-gravitational models.
  • Look for phase-locked deviations near the Jovian gradient.
  • Compare orbital fits assuming η = 1 vs. η < 1 + ℛ > 0.

Consolidated Observational Tests

The March 2026 window is treated as a compact, falsifiable test for the Structural Plenitude interpretation of 3I/ATLAS:

  • Does the antitail preserve its geometry as the impedance gradient increases?

Agreement, disagreement or partial alignment with these patterns refines the allowed parameter space for η, ZH and ℛ.
